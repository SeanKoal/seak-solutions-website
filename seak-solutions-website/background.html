BACKGROUND

How I actually got here.

I went to school for automotive engineering first. It didn't fit, and I didn't stick with it long — but looking back, that wasn't really a left turn so much as a wrong turn that got corrected.

What actually fit was always there. I grew up taking computers apart and putting them back together, usually worse than I found them at first. I hosted my own Minecraft servers as a kid, which meant learning networking and server management before I had a word for either one. A cybersecurity course in high school was the first time someone gave that instinct a name and a direction.

So when automotive engineering didn't work out, I didn't pivot — I just went back to what had been true the whole time. I'm now two semesters from a B.S. in Computer Programming and Information Science with a Cybersecurity minor at Farmingdale State College.

Right now, that means a full schedule: Assistant Store Manager at Vitamin Shoppe during the day, classes and certs (A+ now, Network+ and Security+ next) on top of that, and this homelab built in whatever time is left. I'm on Long Island, building all of it from here.

// THE POINT OF ALL OF IT

None of it is for a piece of paper. I wanted to walk into IT or cybersecurity work with something real behind the certs — a network I built, broke, and fixed myself — not just a transcript. This site, and everything linked from it, is that proof.
